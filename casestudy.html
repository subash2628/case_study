Organizational Structure

This company consists of 15 members and it has been implementing team-based organizational structure. A team-based organizational structure creates small teams that focus on delivering one product or service. Every member of the organization is involved in decision making and sharing ideas of their own. Each member has an equal share but most of the financial decisions are made by the CEO. The organization consists of the tech department, marketing department, production department, financial department. Since the company is a small team, the hiring process is also handled by the marketing department.

Personnel Policies

There are numerous laws and regulations which regulate the nature of the relationship between an employee and the organization. They are intended primarily to ensure that everyone is treated fairly and equitably. Since _ focuses on the growth of everyone and democratic work culture, everyone is equal.They particularly operate on project basis. Each project is assigned a leader which is changed periodically in order to develop leadership quality in every employee and all team members are treated equally. The policies developed by an organization depend on the nature and needs of the organization. Here are some personnel policies_:

1. Work Schedule

They often work during day hours. For those who are not free or have to complete any project, also can work during any time as per their comfort.. There is no official opening and closing time of the office. The employees work on a milestone basis i.e. they need to complete certain tasks before the deadline. However, there are no hard constraints on the deadline.The company remains open for six days a week. Employees can take leave for their personal reasons but they may be required to inform them beforehand so that it wouldn’t hamper the current project.

2. Financial Management

To discuss income distribution and how much money should be used for the company, they regularly arrange a meeting.  They also talk about other duties, such as financial reporting, capital expenditures, and budget management. They discuss how the profit can be maximized, Are there any projects that can be completed at cheaper cost by outsourcing?, and so on.

 3. Data Practices

They keep security records on the business's public disclosures, internal communications, use of data, staff data, client contracts, etc. They maintain a record of past mistakes they have made in the database and approach they adopted for resolving the issue. It acts as the company’s asset for future decisions and success.

Manpower

  • Manpower planning

Manpower planning is the process of estimating or forecasting the number of employees (with different skills) needed for a project over a period of time. The Manpower  planning also includes information such as when and how to hire new employees. The whole process is carried out taking into account the purpose of the organization, expectations for the future business and changing technological trends.

Every important part or department of the company has to control the human resources of the company. With any possible event such as a change in industrial dynamics, an increase in trade, demands increase the need for better skills and resources for a particular technology and organization needs to update its current strategy to handle these changes.

  • Recruitment and selection of manpower

During our case study while conducting an interview with the team,  the team of _ was fully satisfied with the number of members and workflow. However, they were searching for new ways and techniques to increase the portfolio of the company.  There were some vacancies at the time of interview with the team, related to the Data, videography and animation field.

The steps of manpower planning are:

Understanding the existing workforce:

The main authority of the organization such as the CEO and the coordinator has to thoroughly understand the manpower available with the company. They should examine the background, skill set, qualification, location etc. of the entire workforce so that they have a good idea regarding the pool of talent which the company has. _ maintains the record of every employee, their skillset, their area of expertise and their areas of interest and assigns them to the team based on their previous performances.

Forecasting for the future:

Companies must understand the future trend and which type of employees would be best suited for their organization as business requirements change on a regular basis. As a result, businesses must examine, evaluate, and forecast the type of employee workforce they desire in the coming years.

Selection of manpower:

Manpower planning, depending on the needs of the business, results in a much more well-thought-out recruitment and selection pattern. This is entirely dependent on forecasts and business requirements. As a result, candidates with superior qualifications, skill sets, experience, and so on are chosen as employees to best meet the company's future needs.

Training the employees:

Employees who are a part of the organization are trained to have the best skills, knowledge and understanding about the current job as well as the future requirements.As per the interview with the CEO of the company _  provides initial training to all the employees joining their company. The employees also receive training and develop the skill set on need basis regularly. At _, the leader of the team always tries to help his/her team-mates in case of any problems.        

Job Evaluation

At _, if they want to hire a new person in their company then they seek some skill, experience and characteristics in their new employee. At first, they look at the previous projects done by the person being hired and the tools used in his/her project. As per the CEO while hiring they ask the person to make a short video about 2 minutes showcasing their skills. Sometimes, they are asked to perform a particular task related to the job and are evaluated on the basis of the task they have performed.They try to assess if a person has acquired enough practical knowledge and has skills for the job.

They also look at the person's attitude. They want to see honesty, integrity, and a "can-do" attitude, as well as the learning curve, which determines how quickly a skill will be acquired and applied on the job. They look for a learning attitude and will to grow every single day in a person. Lastly, the most important skill they look for is good communication skills and listening skills.

Merit Rating

Merit Rating is a systematic process for measuring the performance of the employees in terms of job requirements. In order to compare individual workers within a work group in terms of their personal characteristics or shortcomings and the requirements of their particular professions, it employs a variety of rating techniques. The fact that people have different skills and aptitudes is well-established. Even if they had access to the same training and educational resources, their disparities would still exist to some level because they are part of nature.

The objectives of merit rating followed by the company _ can be described as below:

1. They are aware of the challenges faced by workers in a variety of jobs.

2.They evaluate the good and bad aspects of employees' work and then make        suggestions for improvement.

3. They help in wage and salary administrations and take decisions about incentives and increments to be given to the workers.

4. They consider employees/workers for promotions, rewards, and layoffs, among other things.

5. They assess employees' skill and training capabilities and assist in the development of appropriate training and development programs for workers.

Wages and Incentives

Wages are the compensation provided to employees for the physical and mental labor they put into the production process. The corporation pays the wages of the employees and developers. The company does every financial activity very systematically following legal procedures. The company has set up separate accounts for providing facilities like food, lunch and for performing recreational activities. They set out the terms and conditionals in an agreement with clients before starting any project. The clients as per the agreement by the client make the payment after the completion of their project. They have defined policy for clients as well as employees.

Marketing

Marketing is the activity set of institutions and processes for creating, communicating , delivering and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large. In short, marketing involves sales and advertising. Marketing is used with the following objectives: Product development, Product pricing, Sales Distribution, Creating revenue, and Promotion. The company mainly advertises through social media by creating advertising videos. This company mainly focuses on digital marketing techniques. As most of the clients know about this company through digital platforms, the marketing strategy includes SEO, tiktok,  facebook and other social media.

Drawbacks/Recommendation

The current organization structure could create bottlenecks in the decision making process, can be counterproductive and could create a problem in meeting organization’s objectives.so, creating a governing structure could help to provide clear guidelines for members on how to proceed. This will help to create an outline on who will be responsible for what within an organization. Also, the company uses a traditional approach for planning tasks, keeping records, and making the company's code of conduct. It would be better if we use modern software for record keeping, an approach like Gantt’s chart for planning tasks.
